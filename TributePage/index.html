Colin L. Powell

A U.S. Leader & Pioneer Statesman

Colin L. Powell was a U.S. General, Chairman of the Joint Chiefs of Staff and U.S. Secretary of State.

Here's a timeline of Colin L. Powell's life:

  • 1937: Born in New York City
  • 1958: Commissions as a Second Lieutenant in the U.S. Army
  • 1962: Serves first tour in the Vietnam War
  • 1971: Earns Master of Business Administration from Georgetown University
  • 1979: Named Senior Military Assistant to the Deputy Secretary of Defense
  • 1987: Appointed National Security Advisor for President Ronald Reagan
  • 1989: Named Chairman of the Joint Chiefs of Staff
  • 1991: Takes charge of operations during Desert Shield and Desert Storm
  • 2001: Appointed, confirmed, and sworn in as the Secretary of State
  • 2019: Powell is given the Lincoln Medal by the Ford's Theatre Society
  • 2021: Powell passes away from complications related to COVID-19
“Everyone who worked with General Powell appreciated his clarity of thought, insistence on seeing all sides, and ability to execute. And although he’d be the first to acknowledge that he didn’t get every call right, his actions reflected what he believed was best for America and the people he served.”
-- President Barack Obama (tribute to Colin Powell)
